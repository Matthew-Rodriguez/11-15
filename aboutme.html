Zina Saro-Wiwa

Artist Statement

01 Artist Statement



Through her art, Zina Saro-Wiwa radically transforms our conception of environmentalism. For her this implicates not just worries about greenhouse emissions and oil pollution, but also emotional landscapes, "emotional hygiene”, spiritual ecosystems, the transcendent power of food and the politics of land.

She uses multiple strategies: video art, movie and documentary making, writing, curatorial projects and food initiatives to explore her place in the world and to construct a more thorough and more integrated concept of environmentalism. One that imagines more fully how we truly live in the world. She says: “I am an artist of African origin but I live between many cultures, worlds and psychic dimensions. I live in a very particular place as a result of my personal history, spiritual heritage, heterodox politics and cultural reality and I think my work has long been about trying to find my place in the worlds on this earth. For this reason environment means much more than global warming. Invisible ecosystems matter just as much as the material realm.” Saro-Wiwa views our relationship to the earth and its fruits as a powerful portal. Her potent and often hypnotic works utilizes performance of apparently mundane activities such as eating, kissing, praying crying. But for Saro-Wiwa there is beauty, codes and powerful information in the everyday.

Through her investigations into the notion of place and placelessness, Zina Saro-Wiwa invents genres and breaks boundaries. She is engaged in world-building through the institutions she creates that perform as living sculptures and living ideas that challenge accepted orthodoxies. Instinctively heterodox in her outlook, she tests the utility and application of art practice and uses art to challenge both herself, her place in the world and her audience.
